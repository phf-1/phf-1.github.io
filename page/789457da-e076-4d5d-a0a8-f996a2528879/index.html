Abstract.

The objective is to analyse the publication of Carl Hewitt: Actors and continuous functionals in terms of questions and answers. Compare the ideas to the ones already analyse in analysis of publications and avoid to add duplicated ones.

What is the the continuity criterion for graphs of functions studied in the Scott-Strachey model of computation?

What is the graph of a function?

The graph of a function is simply the set of all input output pairs for the function.

What is the relation between a procedure p and a the notion of continuous functional?

We can prove that the graph of any physically realizable procedure p that behaves like a mathematical function as the limit of a continuous functional F such that:

\text{graph}(p) = \bigcup_{i \in ℕ} F^i(\{\})

In other words the graph of p is the limit of the n-fold compositions of F with Itself beginning with the empty graph.
