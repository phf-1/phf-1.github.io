What is a Future?

The evaluator does this by creating and returning for each subexpresslon a f uture, which is a promise to deliver the value of that subexpression at some later time, if it has a value. Each future can evaluate its subexpression independently and concurrently with other futures because it is created with its own evaluator process, which is dedicated to evaluating its subexpression. When the value of a future is needed explicitly, e.g. by the primitive function + , the evaluator of the subexpression may or may not be complete. If it Is complete, the future’s value is immediately available; if not, the requesting process is forced to wait until the evaluation of the subexpression is done.
